Eligibility rules exist to keep the league fair and to ensure that only currently enrolled college students compete. As team manager, it is your responsibility to verify that every player on your roster meets these requirements **before** match day.

<Warning>
  Fielding an ineligible player — even unknowingly — can result in a match forfeit or disqualification. Do not wait until match night to check. Run through this checklist at the start of each week.
</Warning>

---

## Player eligibility requirements

Every player on your roster must satisfy **all four** of the following:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Verified .edu email" icon="envelope-circle-check">
    The player must have added and verified a .edu email address on their C3 Esports profile. Free webmail addresses (Gmail, Outlook, Yahoo) do not qualify.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Linked Discord account" icon="discord">
    The player must have connected their Discord account via the profile settings. This is how staff and opponents identify players.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Not on another active roster" icon="ban">
    Players can only be on **one** registered team roster at a time. Being on a second team's roster — even in a different division — is not permitted.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Currently enrolled" icon="graduation-cap">
    Players must be actively enrolled at an accredited college or university. Graduated students, students on leave, or non-enrolled individuals are not eligible.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Checking eligibility on your roster page

Your team's roster page shows an eligibility status for each player. Look for:

- **Green checkmark** — player meets all requirements
- **Yellow warning** — player is missing something (hover or click to see what)
- **Red X** — player is ineligible and cannot compete

## Proof of enrollment

Staff operate on an **honor system** but reserve the right to request proof of enrollment at any time — particularly if a dispute is filed or if a player's eligibility is questioned.

Acceptable proof includes:

- A current class schedule (screenshot from your school's student portal)
- A tuition payment receipt for the current semester
- An official enrollment verification letter from your registrar

<Note>
  Staff will contact the player directly via Discord or email if proof of enrollment is requested. Players who cannot provide proof within 48 hours of the request may be ruled ineligible for that match week.
</Note>

---

## Graduate students and part-time students

Graduate students and part-time students are eligible as long as they are officially enrolled and have a valid .edu email. There is no GPA or credit hour minimum — eligibility is based on enrollment status only.

---

## What happens if an ineligible player competes

If it is discovered that an ineligible player played in a match:

1. The match result may be overturned in the opposing team's favor.
2. The ineligible player may be removed from the roster by staff.
3. Repeated violations may result in suspension of the team manager.

If you discover a player on your own roster is ineligible **before** a match, remove them or have them fix their account immediately. Proactively reporting issues is always better than staff discovering them during a dispute.

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="A player's school uses a .edu domain but through a third-party provider — does it count?">
    Yes. What matters is that the email address ends in `.edu` and that the player receives the verification email there. If the school has officially issued the address, it qualifies.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="A player graduated mid-season. Are they still eligible?">
    No. Eligibility requires current enrollment. A player who graduates during the season is no longer eligible for remaining matches. Remove them from the roster or contact staff to discuss an exception for playoff qualification purposes.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can a player be on a roster in one division and a sub for another team?">
    No. Players can only appear on one registered roster at a time, regardless of division or sub status. Emergency sub policies are handled by staff on a case-by-case basis — see [Roster Rules](/rules/roster-rules).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="A player has two .edu emails (e.g., they transferred). Which one do they use?">
    Either is acceptable as long as it is a valid, active .edu address they can receive mail at. They only need to verify one.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
